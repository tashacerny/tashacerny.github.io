Tasha Cerny is a Google-certified Project Manager with 10+ years of

## Case Study: Cross-Channel Campaign Management
**Creative Teaching Press | 2022–2025**

### The Challenge
Creative Teaching Press needed to manage marketing campaigns for over 
100 new products per year across multiple digital channels — blog, 
email, social media, and newsletter — while maintaining a consistent 
brand voice and hitting launch deadlines.

### The Approach
Tasha stepped in as Project Manager to bring structure and strategy 
to the full campaign lifecycle. She built and maintained editorial 
calendars across all channels, coordinated cross-functional teams 
around product launch timelines, and developed a content cadence 
that kept all channels aligned and on schedule. Noticing underperforming 
email metrics, she led a full email performance audit to identify gaps 
and opportunities.

### The Results
- Directed end-to-end digital marketing campaigns for 100+ products annually
- Developed and maintained editorial strategy across blog, newsletter, and social
- Email performance audit resulted in a **25% increase in click-through rate**

## Case Study: Writers' Room Production Management
**TV Production, Los Angeles | 2015–2024**

### The Challenge
Writers' rooms on TV productions are fast-moving, high-pressure 
environments where creative and logistical demands collide. Five 
separate series needed someone to keep internal workflows running 
smoothly while bridging the gap between creative teams and production.

### The Approach
Over nearly a decade, Tasha served as the organizational backbone 
of writers' rooms across five TV series. She managed internal 
workflows, kept projects on schedule, liaised with production teams 
to align creative execution with budget and deadlines, and contributed 
creatively by pitching content solutions that addressed narrative 
challenges.

### The Results
- Successfully managed production workflows across five TV series
- Consistently delivered on-time, on-budget creative execution
- Built strong cross-functional relationships between writers and production
